SLOWDIVE

Slowdive formed in Reading, Berkshire, England, in late 1989. The core of the band were childhood friends Rachel Goswell and Neil Halstead, both on vocals and guitar.

The name “Slowdive” was inspired by a dream that Nick Chaplin had plus a conversation with Goswell that referenced a song titled “Slowdive” by Siouxsie and the Banshees.

They released some EPs and built a reputation tied to the burgeoning “shoegaze” scene characterised by textured guitars, ethereal vocals and a somewhat introspective atmosphere.

SLOWDIVE – Pygmalion

Music fans largely associated the breakup of Slowdive in 1995 with the disappearance of the shoegazing genre. Its last sign of life was Pygmalion (before their reunion in 2014), and after Just For A Day and Souvlaki, it was a completely different and more abstract album — and one of the most important shoegaze albums of all time. While the band had tried to incorporate more structure into their songs on Souvlaki, they began integrating more elements of ambient electronic music on Pygmalion. Tracks like “Blue Skied An’ Clear” and “Crazy For You” show that the songs are still there, somewhere — they’re simply buried under sounds more abstract than before.
